Car Specifications

Top Speed

380 km/h

Formula 1 cars typically reach top speeds of around 360–370 km/h (223–230 mph) during races, with the highest official race speed record being 372.6 km/h (231.52 mph)

Engine

Hybrid V6 Turbo

Modern Formula 1 cars use highly advanced 1.6-liter V6 hybrid turbocharged engines, producing over 1,000 hp (combined) at up to 15,000 RPM. These power units, mandated since 2014, utilize MGU-K and MGU-H energy recovery systems.

Acceleration

0-100 in 2.4s

Modern Formula 1 cars are among the fastest accelerating vehicles on earth, reaching 0–60 mph (km/h) in roughly 1.7 to 2.6 seconds. They achieve 0–100 mph (km/h) in around 4 seconds or less. Acceleration is limited by traction rather than power, using hybrid engines with over 1,000 HP and immense aerodynamic downforce to achieve these speeds.

Power Unit (Hybrid Engine)

350 kW (~470 hp)

Total output is close to 1000 brake horsepower (bhp). MGU-H (heat recovery from turbo) has been removed for simplicity. Uses 100% sustainable drop-in fuel (E100 or advanced biofuels) — much greener. Battery energy recovery is more powerful, especially under braking, for stronger acceleration and overtaking.

Active Aerodynamics

Old DRS system

Drivers can switch between two modes: Low-drag mode (straights) — wings flatten for higher top speed. High-downforce mode (corners) — wings adjust for maximum grip.
